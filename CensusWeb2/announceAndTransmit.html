Announce and Transmit: A Century of Congressional Apportionment

Announce and Transmit An Enduring Tradition Primary Sources

ANNOUNCE

Every 10 years, the U.S. Census Bureau counts each person in the United States, fulfilling a duty inscribed by the founders in the Constitution of the United States. For nearly a century now, the Census Bureau and the Department of Commerce have followed the count by publicly ANNOUNCING the total numbers of persons in each state and the number of representatives each state will have in the next decade's House of Representatives.

TRANSMIT

The Census Bureau and Department of Commerce also TRANSMIT that packet of numbers through the President to Congress according to deadlines fixed in law by Congress.

November 2, 1950

TRANSMISSION TO PRESIDENT AND RELEASE TO PUBLIC

Secretary Sawyer, along with Director Peel and Deputy Director Eckler, delivered the apportionment numbers to President Truman, and the Commerce Department and Census Bureau announced the official counts for the 1950 census

January 9, 1951

TRANSMISSION TO CONGRESS

President Truman transmitted the apportionment numbers to Congress

January 1951

TRANSMISSION TO THE GOVERNORS

The Clerk of the U.S. House of Representatives sent certificates to the governors of each state, indicating the number of representatives each state was entitled to in the reapportioned 83rd Congress.

DECEMBER 21, 2010

TRANSMISSION TO PRESIDENT AND RELEASE TO PUBLIC

Secretary of Commerce Gary Locke delivered the apportionment counts to President Obama, 10 days before the statutory deadline of Dec. 31.

JANUARY 5, 2011

TRANSMISSION TO CONGRESS

President Obama transmitted the apportionment counts to the 112th Congress during the first week of its first regular session in January. The reapportioned Congress was the 113th, which would convene in January 2013

JANUARY 12, 2011

TRANSMISSION TO THE GOVERNORS

The Clerk of the U.S. House of Representatives sent certificates to the governors of each state, indicating the number of seats representatives each state was entitled to in the reapportioned 113th Congress, which convened in January 2013.
