Announce and Transmit:

A Century of Congressional Apportionment

Announce and Transmit An Enduring Tradition Primary Sources

Once the 2020 census has been completed, the Census Bureau, the Department of Commerce, and the President of the United States will be expected to ANNOUNCE the total populations of each state and the number of representatives each state is due in the next House of Representatives. The President will then TRANSMIT that packet of figures to Congress. This website explains the historical precedents governing this tradition of ANNOUNCE and TRANSMIT.

Announce

Every 10 years, the U.S. Census Bureau counts each person in the United States, fulfilling a duty inscribed by the founders in the Constitution of the United States. For nearly a century now, the Census Bureau and the Department of Commerce have followed the count by publicly ANNOUNCING the total numbers of persons in each state and the number of representatives each state will have in the next decade's House of Representatives.

Transmit

The Census Bureau and Department of Commerce also TRANSMIT that packet of numbers through the President to Congress according to deadlines fixed in law by Congress.

Dec. 21, 2010

Transmission to President and Release to Public

Secretary of Commerce Gary Locke delivered the apportionment counts to President Obama, 10 days before the statutory deadline of Dec. 31.

Jan. 5, 2011

Transmission to Congress

President Obama transmitted the apportionment counts to the 112th Congress during the first week of its first regular session in Jan.. The reapportioned Congress was the 113th, which would convene in Jan. 2013

Jan. 12, 2011

Transmission to the Governors

The Clerk of the U.S. House of Representatives sent certificates to the governors of each state, indicating the number of seats representatives each state was entitled to in the reapportioned 113th Congress, which convened in Jan. 2013.

Dec. 28, 2000

Transmission to President and Release to Public

“Mineta, Shapiro and Census Bureau Director Kenneth Prewitt” transmitted the Census 2000 apportionment counts to President Clinton. Commerce Secretary Norman Y. Mineta, Under Secretary for Economic Affairs Robert J. Shapiro and Census Bureau Director Kenneth Prewitt released the first set of Census 2000 numbers — the resident population and Congressional apportionment totals for each state — at a news briefing at the National Press Club.

Jan. 4, 2001

Transmission to Congress

President Clinton sent his report on the apportionment population of each state as of April 1, 2000, and the number of Representatives to which each state would be entitled.

Jan. 2001

Transmission to the Governors

Dec. 26, 1990

Transmission to President and Release to Public

Mosbacher delivered the apportionment numbers to President Bush. A press briefing was held at 11 a.m. on the same day.

Jan. 7, 1991

Transmission to Congress

Congress received a communication from the President of the United States, transmitting his report on the apportionment population for each State and the number of Representatives to which each State would be entitled.

Dec. 1, 1940

Release to Public

The Census Bureau tabulated the final 1940 census figures.

Jan. 8, 1941

Transmission to Congress

President Roosevelt transmitted the apportionment numbers to Congress. Note: Two sets of numbers were transmitted. One used the method known as the method of major fractions, which was the method used in the last preceding apportionment, and the other used the method of equal proportions.
